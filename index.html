“Saddle the Madness”: How to Make Advertising Five Times More Effective

An English translation of the original article, preserving its illustrations and video embeds.

Translated from Russian · Original published by AIN.UA

Illustrations: Yura Rusovsky, Zhenya Velichev.

In December, the annual presentation of the Effie Awards took place in Kyiv, the most prestigious award in the world of marketing communications, which since 1968 has been awarded in 47 countries for the main achievement in the field - efficiency.

The jury, which consisted of 200 top managers of leading Ukrainian and international companies, reviewed 360 successful advertising campaigns. And, after weighing the results, it determined the best.

We believe that the key to unlocking the magical power of communications lies in the blending of strategy and creativity. This is one discipline. One process. Creative process.

Adequacy when working with brands is a carefully controlled madness. We call it: riding the madness.

Creativity helps to marry functional purpose and emotional resonance. Achieve a business goal in such a way as to make your brand stand out as much as possible and “make a splash” in the category.

Creativity is the fuel for business and below we will tell you how we use it in marketing.

Let's start with the fact that viewers make effective advertising. They either like her or they don't. It's up to them to decide. Marketers can only guess which levers are best suited here.

If people share your advertising on social networks and react to it, this is the first sign of its effectiveness. They are talking about you, which means they know your brand, it is interesting.

In textbooks this is called word of mouth. The crown of efficiency and sales growth. Word of mouth is a powerful driver that forces us to buy or not buy what we have noticed. Between 20% and 50% of all purchasing decisions are due to word of mouth. And it’s true: if your friend said that he really liked one store, you like it too. And you don't even need advertising.

If people talk about your brand for a long time and interest does not drop, then you have brilliant marketers. Or maybe it's you. In general, congratulations, your customers have already begun to turn into your fans. And you become a brand icon.

For more details about what other brands there are, see material for AIN.UA.

Without hundreds, then thousands of advocates, your brand will never be truly strong. Therefore, you need advertising primarily not in order to sell your product here and now, but in order to build relationships with enough people so that they turn into your agents. Invest your time, effort and money into this.

Your product is sold not by your advertising, but by people who have never worked for your company. The more advocates your brand has to support its story and protect it, the more powerful it is.

To get your first lawyer, you need to gain attention, then trust. Your credibility is something that your competitors cannot copy.

For it to appear between you and people, what Americans call connection and belonging must happen. After that, you are accepted into the club: you find yourself in the world of people. And sales begin to grow rapidly. The difficulty is that people’s attention cannot be bought for any money, it can only be earned.

Round One - My Attention

I live in my smartphone and use it as a mechanism that relieves me of the stress of mundane everyday life. Please take into account that I am a very lazy person, I quickly lose interest and switch. Most of the time (90% of my time there) I'm surfing the internet doing nothing and want to watch something that entertains me. And I have already learned not to see advertising automatically.

My finger has developed a skip reflex. Therefore, if you want me to stop on your advertisement, catch my eye from the very first second. If this is a video, then it should slow me down literally with the first frame. If my thumb wasn’t interested in “what’s next?” - he will not spare anyone, neither women, nor children, nor Dalmatians.

Thumb-stopping content

80% of us watch YouTube videos before making a purchase. 70% of viewing time is on smartphones. Therefore, you need to learn to “think with a smartphone.”

50% percent of all videos on YouTube have less than five hundred views. Only 0.3% can boast one million (or higher). 300 hours of new content are uploaded to YouTube every minute, and this number is growing. It's very difficult to get attention these days. This makes it more interesting. You have to belong on YouTube. Not a guest. And feel at home in the world of mobile devices.

Your advertisement should give me a reason to pay attention to it. The fact that you want me to buy your product and show it beautifully from all sides is not a reason.

The reason may be something that is out of the ordinary order of things. And not necessarily with its nonconformism or shockingness. For example, the reason to watch the first season of The Mandalorian was a photo of Baby Yoda that we saw on Twitter.

Round Two - My Trust

Why on earth should I trust any brand? I bought it and forgot it. But it's not that interesting. Our imagination searches for someone to fall in love with.

For a long time they choose only those brands from which they feel real emotions. And they never choose with reason.

I will never fall in love with a body lotion or aftershave or antiperspirant, but I will fall in love with a brand that sells them because it hits my emotional chord.

We think that we are rational beings. We are confident that we make logical decisions based on evidence. But that's not true. We don't do that.

In many important areas of our lives, the mind plays a microscopic role. Instead, we are driven by unconscious desires. We are drawn to the beautiful, the extravagant and the absurd. Therefore, if you want to gain people's trust, you must bypass reason. The best ideas don't make rational sense: they make you feel more than they make you think.

Trust is born with empathy. We think that Tom Hanks is a very good person who can be trusted as a childhood friend, because we empathized with his best roles.

Empathy is intuitive or conscious empathy for the current emotional state of another person. Therefore, it is good when there is someone alive behind the brand. It could be the owner. Or an actor. But maybe a meerkat.

Who is the current face of your brand? You may be a company with thousands of employees around the world and a large board of directors. "So what?" - people will tell you. Show your face. Let's return to advertising effectiveness. And to the fact that in 2020 it may seem to you a false answer to all the questions on the way to it.

Captured by digital marketing

The era of digital marketing has arrived. We have learned to target messages with millimeter precision and follow the target audience on the heels of the Internet. But more and more often on Instagram I click “Hide ad” for the reason “I see it too often.” And it's annoying. Advertising has become even more intrusive and has learned to catch up with us in the most unexpected places, but this has not made us any better. She became omnipresent.

Marketers have a false sense that they now have secret knowledge that allows them to whisper directly into people's ears.

You can know everything about your audience, down to the slightest nuances of behavior during the day, but if your advertising is banal, no matter how many times you slip it in, there will be no effect.

You may be data gods, but as David Abbott said: “Shit that arrives at the speed of light is still shit.”

It seems that clients and even agencies are more interested in media placement than the content itself. From these complex algorithms of interweaving program data, digital technologies, CX, UX, etc., large and beautiful presentations are obtained. It all looks like an impressive plan of action. But priorities in advertising have not changed and will never change. Ideas and the human factor will always be at the forefront. Technology will remain only a means. Therefore, if there is not a drop of creativity in this regard, it is doomed.

Creativity is still king

After studying 500 case studies in 2017, Nielsen provided a comprehensive analysis of what influences advertising effectiveness - creativity, reach, targeting, relevance, context and how much of a brand you are.

Key takeaway: Creativity is the undisputed leader in sales drivers (47%). This is what people respond to best, what they talk about, what they give their attention and trust to.

What's also interesting: For large cross-media campaigns, reach is still primarily through television.

Television content outperforms digital content in terms of quality. The picture is this: if on TV the quality of creative is kept at the “average” level, then the Internet has a wider range from “much higher” to “much lower.”

Moreover, there is noticeably more of the second. In other words: on the Internet there is simply a wave of advertising trash with a bet on placement. And without relying on creativity. And this is the road to nowhere. Because it distances us from people, but should bring us closer.

Yes, understanding purchasing cycles and timing of advertising closer to purchases can significantly increase sales. But, again, subject to good creativity. Otherwise, people will brush off your advertising and only get annoyed. Why buy from those who annoy you?

Brand is a symbol of trust

Today, each of us is a marketer. Everyone has their own brand, their own audience. This is our social media account. In the world of corporate brands, everything is exactly the same. There are those that are interesting to follow and those that we are absolutely indifferent to.

And, if how public to be is a personal matter for everyone, there are no options with corporate brands. You may be comfortable with your 60 subscribers, the number of which has not grown for the third year, but for your company’s brand this is death.

Why aren't people interested in him? Why aren’t his posts shared on their pages? Why don't they comment or react?

The first reason is that they don’t notice him. He didn't deserve people's attention. Didn't give them any reason. He's the same as everyone else. Actually, it is not a brand yet. After all, people haven’t made it like that yet.

Yes, a company can exist without a brand. But it's like living in a plywood house. How to quickly determine whether you have what can be called a brand?

Test: Your Brand Minus Commodity

Take your brand and the category in which it is presented. For example: Bulbashko™ and sweet carbonated drinks.

Next, describe your brand using any words that come to mind. But keep in mind that people don't see your brand the same way you do. So try to do it for them.

Let's imagine that you are a fairly successful company that produces products under the Bulbashko™ brand. You have been advertising for several years now. How do you think people see you?

  • Funny
  • Family
  • Holiday
  • Delicious
  • Quality
  • Friends
  • Bright
  • Positive

Then we also describe commodity, the general equivalent of the product in our category. We have such a strong player that it has already become the personification of the category - Coca Cola. The Coca-Cola logo is the most recognizable in the world. 94% of all inhabitants of the Earth can guess the colors of this brand. Therefore we can describe it.

  • Funny
  • Family
  • Holiday
  • Delicious
  • Quality
  • Friends
  • Bright
  • Positive

Plus

  • Santa Claus
  • Polar bears

We see that the same thing happened. Coca Cola even has two points more.

What does it mean? That your brand doesn’t exist yet. You invest in commodity advertising and advertise Coca Cola, which spends $3.3 billion annually on marketing even without you. Don't invest in commodity advertising. Create your brand.

  • Funny
  • Family
  • Holiday
  • Delicious
  • Quality
  • Friends
  • Bright
  • Positive
  • The one who is for same-sex marriage

Brands change people's attitudes towards commodities. Twitter has changed the way we view news channels. Uber to taxi. Airbnb for hotels. Nike for sporting goods.

Brand pioneer

To increase the effectiveness of your advertising, be a pioneer with your brand. Be the first to say what has already been heard.

Only those advertisements that disrupt the normal order of things are remembered. Right now, Marvel Studios is filming a blockbuster where a superhero will appear - transgender.

Marvel CEO Kevin Feige commented: “We want our films to be a reflection of our audience, so that everyone around the world recognizes themselves on screen.”

This is a great aspiration if you want to build a strong brand. Be the first to tell the truth and help her break through in every possible way. In bad and ineffective advertising, the truth is always primitively embellished.

Together with goods, we buy a small set of feelings and emotions. This is what we imagined for ourselves. Emotional characteristics always prevail over product characteristics.

Medicine doesn't sell drugs, it sells trust. The Lottery sells hope (that could be me!) Tiffany sells the meaning of someone to you, Facebook sells belonging, and Wholefoods sells care and love for yourself and nature. LEGO sells me on its mission: to inspire and develop the “builders of tomorrow.” Innocent juices sell me my desire to do something good for myself today.

This is why brands are created, to help people figure out for themselves why they need your product.

Bulge vs Brand Image 2020

If you choose between brand image or prominence, you should always strive for the second.

Convexity is the number one task for any brand in 2020.

Let's look at the market through the eyes of a buyer. What does he see? Every bank wants to have an image of a company that can be trusted. Any food brand is the image of a company with impeccable quality. And so on. But this makes people bored as hell. They are looking for a brand that they want to fall in love with, which is more interesting.

When monobank and its cat appeared in such a mothball category as banking, everyone immediately paid attention, and soon trust appeared. Mono is now the fastest growing neobank in the world, adding 4,500 new customers every day.

Therefore, I would like to appeal to all brand managers. Don't panic too much about brand image. Don't waste your life on semantic perfection and complete control over all touchpoints. Instead, try to be there all the time. Your task: to become a part of people's lives.

One meme from your bank can be more effective than three advertising campaigns about the convenience and safety of using the product. Because it doesn't look like commercial content. Memes always speak the language of people.

Always be the most noticeable, even when you make mistakes.

Today, every brand should have its own emotional codes. Build your brand on emotions. Just because the whole world knows about the existence of your super-technological product does not make it a bestseller. Buyers may generally pass by. Google Glasses didn't take off because people looked ridiculous wearing them and this problem was not addressed in advertising. No emotions arose.

If your brand book, brand policies, or any other rules prevent you from being the most visible in your category, then those rules work against you, reducing the impact of your brand and the effectiveness of its advertising. Sooner or later he will deflate.

Interest in you should be alive, and not one that needs to be maintained by an artificial respiration apparatus.

So approach and cross the border, walk on thin ice, take risks - this is what is expected of you. In the modern world of brand management, voicing your position, even the stupidest one, is already becoming more important than the position itself. People are already fed up with brands, missions and promises, they are no longer interested in what you say in your advertising, what matters is how unusual you make it.

Time for scams

Kidalt (kid+adult) is an adult who retains his childhood and youthful hobbies.

The word was first recorded in 1985 in The New York Times to describe men 30 years of age and older who are interested in cartoons, fantasy, computer games and useless, but beautiful and often expensive gadgets. 30 years have passed and today this term can rightfully be considered one of the main world trends.

We are bored of living in the real world, we want to live in an imaginary one. Like children.

I recently came across study multi-billion dollar video game industry. What's most interesting: 60% of Americans play every day and the average age of a gamer is 34 years old.

Psychologists explain the “Peter Pan syndrome” as a relatively mild, superficial form of escape from reality into the world of illusions - escapism. And with the development of technology, we began to run away from reality more and more into the world of illusions. This is exactly what you do when you pick up your phone first thing in the morning to scroll through your feed.

But most importantly, the world of childhood is a world of the triumph of uncontrolled creativity. Children create first, and therefore think “why.” And they always do it very naturally and easily.

Armed with this knowledge, marketers can increase the effectiveness of their advertising. Create more than you can logically explain. Be creative like children.

Mickey Mouse Smoker

Our generation likes to romanticize life and at the same time treats it ironically. Fooling around, being cynical and sweet at the same time.

We love naughty brands, driven by children's naive curiosity and creativity. Daring and self-ironic. The Mickey Mouse of a healthy person is no longer interesting, we are interested in the flaws.

Any creator is a bit of a flaw for society. He somehow disagrees with the system. And this behavior model for brands can be very effective.

Attack the status quo, if only because it’s more interesting. You can do this in any category of goods and services, b2b or b2c - it doesn’t matter. Add some roughness to your brand, don't chase the perfect picture. On the contrary, the effectiveness of advertising lies in what the Japanese call Wabi-sabi. In the beauty of what is imperfect, fleeting or unfinished. In the ability to show the beautiful in its nature, genuineness and without excesses. Because our life is wabi-sabi.

Pushing your weak leg forward

In his book " Originals » Adam Grant describes an interesting phenomenon that he calls the “Sarick effect”. The point is that our attention and trust are much more quickly drawn to the shortcomings that our interlocutors voice to us than to their perfection.

Rufus Griscom charmed investors with his brand twice Babbel, talking about the shortcomings of your business. He sold the fledgling company to Disney for $40 million and his presentation slides were about “Why wouldn’t I buy Babbel if I were you?”

I remember when I came to look at a used car and the seller suddenly admitted to me that there was a small dent inside the trunk that I would never have noticed, and which he no longer remembers where it came from. I bought a car. And I was not mistaken. It immediately seemed to me that he was a good person whom I wanted to trust.

Key marketing objective

How to correctly set the task for an advertising campaign? Increase brand love? Become number one in the market?

Change people's attitude towards kefir? These are all “jelly-like” goals that are easy for marketers to get caught up in. You need to be as specific as possible.

Specifically: increase brand awareness among white collar workers from 16% to 25% by March 5, 2020.

Your goals, of course, may differ in essence, but in form they should look like this. The exact target is the capture plan, which already suggests a strategy.

Hacking strategy

Marketing is the science of getting people to buy more from you and at a higher price than from your competitor (who sells the same thing).

But not an exact science. It's more of a talent.

The best marketers are always a combination of strategist and creative. They know what, to whom and how to say in the market to make this happen. They don’t even know, but they feel it.

Therefore, no matter how large your army of analysts, theorists, media planners, and digital experts is, if there is no general in it who knows how to ride the madness, your marketing will be ineffective.

A strategy is an idea that will help you break logic and lead you to your specific goal in the shortest possible way. Once you reach it, set the next one and adjust your strategy.

If you are guided only by logic, then keep in mind that your competitors are guided by the same logic. As a result, you come to the same point.

Showing a girl stunned by the grief of a breakup in a gin advertisement is illogical. Common sense tells us that we need to savor joyful moments in the company of friends and it would be nice to be somewhere on a yacht. But the owner of the Aviation brand. American gin actor Ryan Reynolds doesn't think so.

A good strategist knows how to be illogical. And how to take a detour. His task: to bet on something that goes against conventional wisdom and end up being right. Just like a good investor who chooses unpopular directions, those that seem illogical to others. One of the most successful investors in history, Ray Dalio, says this: speaking at TED - bet against the consensus and be right.

Excellent students and prodigies rarely grow into geniuses. In his book "Excellent Sheep: The Miseducation of the American Elite and the Way to a Meaningful Life ", author William Deresiewicz explains this by the fact that gifted children who take first places in the Olympiads, when they grow up, turn into “flawless sheep” and are unable to create anything fundamentally new. They only know how to flawlessly follow the instructions of their teachers.

Creating something new involves disobedience.

As economist Joseph Schumpeter, who coined the phrase “creative destruction,” noted, originality is the act of creative destruction.

When choosing the main idea for promoting your brand, do not choose the “perfect sheep”. You need the ugly duckling.

Muse

The main character in your story is your customer. Your muse.

How to find her? Traditionally, strategists go this way:

Segmentation - Targeting - Positioning.

Where segmentation involves dividing all the buyers in your category into groups. This is the market size in people. For example, how many Ukrainians actually buy beer? Is it 5 million people, 7, 8, 10? Once we understand the number, we can segment by dividing this pie into shares.

Moreover, we are interested in segmentation based on behavioral characteristics. Age, income, social status are technical information. It is important for us to understand their behavior. (all numbers below are examples, we made them up)

For example, one group buys beer exclusively on Fridays at 7 pm on the way home. Two liters, in a plastic bottle. To get “stuck” before the series on the weekend. Let's call them "Home". There are 1.5 million people like that.

Another group buys beer exclusively at night in nightclubs. 0.33 liters in glass. Let's call them "Fireflies". 500,000.

The third group likes to stock up and buys beer in large packages so that it is always at home. Let's call them "Hamsters". 1 million.

The fourth group does not like bottled beer and, if possible, always chooses draft beer. Let's call them "Renegades". 220,000.

Another group buys beer every day after a hard day at an unloved job, in order, in fact, to forget about this day. "Hard workers." 3 million.

Another interesting group is girls and women who love the taste of beer, but they are a little embarrassed to buy it, because it seems to be considered more of a man's drink. "Beer ladies" 200,000.

There are also “Footballers”, for whom football and beer are one thing, “Crafters”, “Fishermen”. And so on.

When you look at the big picture, do you see which pieces of the pie are bigger, where there is more money, and where you have a better chance of gaining attention and trust, given the activity of competitors? One of the most ingenious segmentations we've seen was done by Facebook strategists for Cambridge Analytica. We know about scandal, which flared up soon, but let's look at this masterpiece.

Americans, Facebook users who had reached voting age, were analyzed, divided into 5 groups, then divided into 14 segments. Based on demographics and behavioral signals. If you see the market the way Trump's team did, your marketing becomes exponentially more effective.

Of course, the ideal is to get all these people. Let, in your case, all beer buyers start buying only your brand. But this is your dream. And to start moving towards your dream, you need to act. You can't be everything to everyone at once. If only because this will require billions of dollars.

Targeting is needed.

Let's imagine that all the customers are a football team, then someone must be its captain. Choose the segment that will become the captain. This will be your target. Start building your brand for this segment.

Work for the captain. Become interesting to him, gain his trust. If you find the keys to the captain, the whole team will definitely notice.

For this to happen, positioning is needed.

This is the essence of your brand. You can call it whatever you like:

  • Brand purpose
  • Brand essence
  • Brand statement
  • Brand DNA
  • Brand belief
  • Brand vision

We prefer one word - position. Because a position either exists or it doesn’t. It's hard to get confused here.

Your brand's position on something in life is what your key segment, your captain, needs to notice. It should be understandable, relevant and interesting to him.

And for this you need creative strategists. Having studied the background, they will come up with a role with which the brand will appear in the life of your key segment. The role should be interesting. She can be brave and inspiring, she can be rebellious or very sweet, the main thing is that she is interesting.

I really like the role of the Benetton brand. This is the largest cosmopolitan among brands. By producing the most ordinary clothes that do not stand out in any way, the brand attacks our attitude towards the “other”. This includes skin color, sexual orientation, religion, and worldview. Standing out not only in its category, but also among all other brands.

His role for me is my conscience. Whenever I see a Benetton advertisement, I also want to do something good.

If you advertise without defining your role, it's like handing out leaflets.

Seems funny. But the vast majority of brands on the market do just that. If you want to increase the effectiveness of your advertising, do not think of commercial content that will “cover” your target audience with the help of algorithms. Do not spray leaflets over the city. Start with the brand. Revive him and place him next to you in the office. Come up with a role for him, give him a point of view, rough edges.

It's time to be bolder. Boomers, Xers, Millennials, Zetas, and Alphas are already expecting and demanding this from brands.

Thank you for your attention. Let's make cool advertising!
